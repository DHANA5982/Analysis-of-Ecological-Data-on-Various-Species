Hypothesis Test

Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test

The two-sample KS test was performed using two samples to find whether the samples come from the same distribution.

From the two box plots, it’s obvious that they did not came from the same distribution.

The cumulative distribution function represents the dissimilarity between the two data sets. Red refers to the CDF of ecological status 11 and green refers to the CDF of ecological status 5. It is apparent, that the two data sets did not overlap consistently. Therefore, these two distributions are dissimilar.

ks.test(eco_status_11$value, eco_status_5$value)
## 
##  Asymptotic two-sample Kolmogorov-Smirnov test
## 
## data:  eco_status_11$value and eco_status_5$value
## D = 0.34601, p-value < 2.2e-16
## alternative hypothesis: two-sided

Here, the two samples are BD11’s CDF and BD5’s CDF. The test produced a p-value smaller than the significant level (0.05) and indicates overwhelming evidence against the null hypothesis. Therefore, the two data sets come from different distributions. The D-statistics is 0.34601, representing level of dissimilarity between the two distributions.

t-test

Boxplots for BD5 change and BD11 change, it seems the median of these two are quite close. So, I assumed that the null hypothesis of both data sets’ mean values are more like same.

t.test(BD5_change$value, mu = avg)
## 
##  One Sample t-test
## 
## data:  BD5_change$value
## t = -1.0958, df = 262, p-value = 0.2742
## alternative hypothesis: true mean is not equal to -0.01589769
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  -0.047644242 -0.006851741
## sample estimates:
##   mean of x 
## -0.02724799

Therefore, here I performed a one-sample t-test and it provides a p-value of 0.274 larger than the threshold level (0.05) and implies that they don’t have sufficient evidence to reject the null hypothesis. In other words, it has enough evidence to claim that the BD5 change mean is equal to the BD11 change mean.

The population mean (mean of BD11 change) is -0.01589769 and the sample mean (mean of BD5 change) is - 0.02724799.

The 95% confidence interval (-0.047644242, -0.006851741) provides the range of possible values for the true mean and suggests that the data is consistent with the possibility that the true mean of both data sets are the same.

From the first plot, the red line refers zero line and the green line refers to model-fitted line. All the data points are spread all over the graph. The plot for residuals is the difference between the actual data set and the model-fitted value. When noticing the data points, though some values are closer to the line, most of the values don’t closely follow the line. Therefore, we can’t precisely say that the residuals are normally distributed. Also, the pattern indicates biasing at one side and the deviated values imply the potential outliers.

The Q-Q plot for residuals seems to be fairly distributed besides having a lighter tail on both sides.
